## General Online Safety

1. **Always verify the sender's email address** before clicking any link or downloading any attachment. Fake emails often use addresses that look almost correct but have small differences (e.g., extra letters, wrong domain).

2. **Never share your passwords, OTPs, or login credentials** with anyone — including people claiming to be from your college, bank, or a company. Legitimate organisations never ask for these via email or message.

3. **Check the URL before entering any information on a website.** Make sure it starts with https:// and that the domain matches the official website exactly.

4. **Do not click on links in unsolicited emails or messages** offering jobs, prizes, loans, or urgent opportunities. Always go directly to the official website to verify.

5. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts including email, GitHub, LinkedIn, and banking apps. This adds an extra layer of security even if your password is compromised.

6. **Use strong, unique passwords** for different accounts. Avoid using your name, date of birth, or common words. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.

---

## Financial and UPI Safety

7. **Never share your UPI PIN, CVV, or bank account number** with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank or payment platform. Receiving money never requires you to enter your PIN.

8. **Be cautious of "collect requests" on UPI apps.** Scammers send fake payment requests and instruct victims to enter their PIN to "receive" money. Entering your PIN on a collect request sends money OUT of your account, not into it.

---

## Reporting Cybercrime

If you or someone you know has been a victim of cybercrime:

- **National Cyber Crime Portal:** cybercrime.gov.in
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** 1930 (available 24x7)
- Report immediately — faster reporting increases the chance of recovering lost funds or data.

---

*Stay alert. Verify before you act. When in doubt, do not click.*
